Overview

CEDR (Centralni evidence dotaci z rozpoctu) is the Czech Central Register of Subsidies operated by the Ministry of Finance. It records all subsidies, grants, and financial contributions provided from the Czech state budget, EU structural funds, and other public financial mechanisms. Since 2010, it has been the authoritative source for tracking how public funds flow from government programs to individual recipients.

For OSINT and investigative analysis, CEDR reveals which companies and organizations receive public funding, the amounts involved, the programs they participate in, and whether funds were properly utilized. This data is critical for detecting subsidy fraud, mapping government-dependent entities, and performing financial due diligence.

Key Features

  • Complete record of national and EU subsidy disbursements
  • Recipient identification by ICO with full payment histories
  • Program-level tracking across all Czech ministries and agencies
  • Open data exports in machine-readable formats

API Access

CEDR provides an open data portal at https://cedropendata.mfcr.cz/ with REST API access to all subsidy records. Data is available in JSON and CSV formats. No authentication is required. The API supports filtering by recipient ICO, subsidy provider, program, date range, and amount thresholds. Bulk data exports are also available for offline analysis.

Use Cases

  • Identifying entities that are heavily dependent on government subsidies
  • Detecting potential subsidy fraud through pattern analysis
  • Due diligence on companies applying for additional public funding
  • Mapping the flow of EU structural funds through Czech intermediaries
  • Investigative journalism on public fund distribution

Integration

Prismatic Platform integrates CEDR as a financial intelligence source in the Czech entity analysis pipeline. Subsidy data is correlated with ARES company records, Registr smluv contracts, and Hlidac statu analytics to build comprehensive pictures of entities' relationships with the state. The adapter tracks subsidy dependency ratios and flags entities with unusually concentrated public funding sources.
