Overview

CUZK (Cesky urad zememericky a katastralni) is the Czech Office for Surveying, Mapping, and Cadastre. It maintains the official land registry (katastr nemovitosti) containing records of all real estate ownership, encumbrances, liens, and easements across the Czech Republic. The cadastral database covers approximately 23 million parcels and is the authoritative source for property ownership verification.

For OSINT investigations, CUZK reveals property holdings of individuals and companies, enabling asset tracing, wealth assessment, and identification of hidden beneficial ownership through property networks.

Key Features

  • Complete property ownership records for the entire Czech Republic
  • Historical ownership chain (list vlastnictvi) for any parcel
  • Geospatial visualization through the CUZK map portal
  • Encumbrance and lien records including mortgages and easements

API Access

CUZK provides several data access channels. The free nahlizeni.cuzk.cz web portal allows basic lookups. Programmatic access is available through the DPMR (Dalkovy pristup do katastru nemovitosti) service requiring registration and per-query fees. Bulk data exports are available through RUIN and VFK formats. The WSDP web services offer structured queries for registered users.

Use Cases

  • Asset tracing and wealth profiling through property ownership analysis
  • Identifying undisclosed property holdings of politically exposed persons
  • Verifying collateral for financial due diligence
  • Mapping corporate real estate portfolios by ICO
  • Detecting property transfers that may indicate asset hiding

Integration

Prismatic Platform integrates CUZK data for asset intelligence and property-based entity resolution. Property ownership records are cross-referenced with ARES company data, Justice.cz beneficial ownership records, and Insolvency Register proceedings to build comprehensive asset profiles. The adapter handles the CUZK-specific data formats and maps them to the platform's unified entity model.
