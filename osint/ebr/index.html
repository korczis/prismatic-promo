EU Business Registry

European Business Registry (EBR) and BRIS - interconnected European company registers providing cross-border entity verification

Type: company
Module: Ebr
Official Website

Capabilities

Cross-Border Company Search EU-Wide Entity Verification Interconnected Register Access Branch Registration Data Cross-Border Merger Tracking Disqualified Director Sharing

Overview

The European Business Registry (EBR) and its successor, the Business Registers Interconnection System (BRIS), provide interconnected access to company registers across EU/EEA member states. Mandated by EU Directive 2012/17/EU (as amended by Directive 2019/1151/EU), BRIS ensures that all national business registers are interconnected, enabling cross-border company searches, automatic notification of branch registrations, and sharing of disqualified director information.

For OSINT purposes, BRIS/EBR provides the critical capability to trace corporate structures across European borders. When a Czech company operates a branch in Germany, or a French entity has a subsidiary registered in the Czech Republic, BRIS connects these registrations. This is essential for pan-European due diligence, beneficial ownership tracing, and understanding multi-jurisdictional corporate groups. The system covers 27 EU member states plus Iceland, Liechtenstein, and Norway.

EUID - European Unique Identifier

Every company accessible through BRIS receives a standardized EUID in the format: {country_code}.{register_code}.{registration_number}. For example, a Czech s.r.o. might have EUID CZ.OR.12345678.

Integration with Prismatic

EBR/BRIS provides the pan-European entity intelligence layer within the Prismatic platform, enabling cross-border corporate structure tracing and multi-jurisdictional due diligence.

# Search across EU registers
{:ok, results} = Ebr.search("Prismatic", country: :all)

Pan-European Due Diligence Pipeline

defmodule PrismaticPerimeter.DueDiligence.PanEuropean do
  @moduledoc """
  Pan-European due diligence combining BRIS data
  with national registers and sanctions screening.
  """

  def eu_entity_check(euid) do
    with {:ok, entity} <- Ebr.get_company(euid),
         {:ok, branches} <- Ebr.branches(euid),
         {:ok, eu_sanctions} <- EuSanctions.search(entity.name),
         {:ok, ofac_sanctions} <- Ofac.search(entity.name) do
      # Enrich with national register data where available
      national_data = case entity.country do
        :CZ -> fetch_czech_data(entity.registration_number)
        :UK -> fetch_uk_data(entity.registration_number)
        _ -> %{source: :bris_only}
      end

      {:ok, %{
        entity: entity,
        branches: branches,
        national_enrichment: national_data,
        sanctions: %{eu: eu_sanctions, ofac: ofac_sanctions},
        cross_border_risk: assess_cross_border_risk(entity, branches),
        jurisdiction_count: count_jurisdictions(entity, branches)
      }}
    end
  end

  defp fetch_czech_data(ico) do
    with {:ok, ares} <- Ares.get_full_details(ico),
         {:ok, justice} <- JusticeCz.get_company(ico),
         {:ok, insolvency} <- InsolvencniRejstrik.check(ico) do
      %{ares: ares, justice: justice, insolvency: insolvency}
    end
  end
end

Access Notes

  • BRIS is primarily accessible through the European e-Justice portal
  • Data depth varies by member state (some share more than minimum requirements)
  • For detailed data, consider querying national registers directly
  • The Czech register provides comprehensive data through BRIS
