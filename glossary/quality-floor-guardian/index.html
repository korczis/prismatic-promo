Quality Floor Guardian

quality

Autonomous quality monitoring system preventing score regression

Definition

The Quality Floor Guardian is an autonomous monitoring system that continuously tracks the platform's quality score and prevents regression below defined thresholds. It operates at four enforcement levels: OPTIMAL (100-99%, monitor only), WARNING (98-99%, alert and investigate), CRITICAL (95-98%, auto-evolution trigger), and EMERGENCY (below 95%, block commits and escalate).

Context in Prismatic

Within the Prismatic Platform, the Quality Floor Guardian runs as part of prismatic_safety and monitors all quality domains via Telemetry events. It detects risk patterns including length() > 0 anti-patterns, new Process.sleep calls, missing @spec annotations, and unsafe map access. The Guardian works alongside Quality DNA for cross-session continuity and the Predictive Pre-Commit system for regression prevention. Current platform quality score: 100/100 (OPTIMAL).

Key Characteristics

  • Four Enforcement Levels: OPTIMAL, WARNING, CRITICAL, and EMERGENCY with progressively stronger interventions
  • Pattern Detection: Identifies risk patterns and anti-patterns that could degrade quality scores
  • Telemetry-Driven: Monitors quality events in real-time through the platform's Telemetry infrastructure
  • Auto-Evolution Trigger: CRITICAL threshold automatically activates self-healing and evolution mechanisms

Related Terms

  • Quality Gates - Enforcement pipeline monitored by the Guardian
  • Quality DNA - Cross-session quality state persistence
  • Quality Debt - Tracked debt points monitored for regression
  • Telemetry - Event system powering Guardian's real-time monitoring
  • SEADF - Evolution framework triggered by Guardian alerts
